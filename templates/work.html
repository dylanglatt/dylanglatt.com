Each of these started with a problem I wanted to solve. I scoped it, built it, and shipped it. The products are live — the process is the point.

Riffd

LLM-Powered Song Analyzer

As a musician, I wanted to understand exactly how a song was built — what key it’s in, how the bass line moves against the chords, what makes the progression work. You needed a trained ear for that. No tool could do it for you.

So I built one. Pick any track: it separates every instrument, detects the key, tempo, and chords, and explains the theory in plain English. Three AI models handle the audio processing; everything runs in the browser.

Argus

Real-Time AI Conflict Intelligence

I wanted what intelligence agencies have — a live feed of conflict events that’s already been filtered and verified. The underlying data is public, but it’s hundreds of thousands of raw, unverified events a day. Nobody had built the infrastructure to make it usable.

So I built it. A five-stage pipeline filters events using rules and AI classification, then cross-references what survives against satellite thermal imagery for independent confirmation. The output is a live map and feed of verified conflict events.
