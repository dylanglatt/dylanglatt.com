Full-stack audio platform · Live at riffdlabs.com

Riffd

Musicians can’t easily learn theory from songs they actually listen to. I needed a tool that didn’t exist — one that connects real music to real theory.

I designed and deployed a platform that takes any song, splits it into its individual parts (vocals, bass, guitar, drums), figures out the musical theory behind how it’s built, and gives you an interactive mixer to hear each part on its own — all in the browser, no downloads required.

OSINT intelligence platform · Live at argusosint.vercel.app

Argus

Open-source conflict data is high-volume and extremely noisy. Raw event feeds are full of false positives — agricultural burns flagged as explosions, crime reports classified as armed conflict, diplomatic statements mixed in with kinetic events. Useful signal is buried.

I built a full OSINT pipeline: live GDELT 2.0 ingestion, a two-tier noise filter that routes ambiguous events to Claude Haiku for article-level evaluation, and satellite corroboration via NASA FIRMS thermal anomaly data cross-referenced against a CIA/DARPA-funded conflict zone index. The frontend is a Blueprint-dark analyst workstation with a Mapbox event map, escalation detection, and live UN OCHA humanitarian briefs by country.
