About Me

Dylan Glatt

New York, NY

I identify problems, understand what they actually require, build the solution, and explain it clearly — to engineers, to executives, to anyone who needs to act on it.

My background is in data analysis and quantitative modeling — building data-driven forecasting systems for GM and ExxonMobil. 48 vehicle-level demand models consolidated into a single executive dashboard. Media effectiveness measured across 13 global markets. The work was cross-functional by default: I built the analytics, managed stakeholders across teams, and delivered results clear enough that anyone could act on them without needing to understand how they were built.

I also led the process redesign of shared marketing mix models across international offices — crashing, undocumented, and creating days of friction across time zones. I standardized the structure, centralized assumptions, introduced change logs, and built workflow automation around the handoffs. Crashes went to zero. Coordination became routine.

The projects here — Riffd and Argus — are examples of how I work: identify a real problem, design the solution, and direct it from concept to production. I use AI as the implementation layer, which means I can move from idea to deployed product alone. The problem identification, architecture, and decisions throughout are mine.

If you’re looking for someone who can operate at the level of the problem, build through ambiguity, and communicate clearly to any audience, let’s talk.
