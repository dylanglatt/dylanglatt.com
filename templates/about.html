About

I'm a builder and operator based in New York. I work on products and analytical systems — things with clear purposes, clean internals, and real uses. Most of what I build starts from a problem I ran into myself and couldn't find a good solution for.

I've worked in quantitative analytics — with Fortune 500 companies like GM, ExxonMobil, Fisher Investments, and Major League Soccer on how to model and optimize multi-million dollar marketing budgets: building the models, surfacing where spend wasn't working, and translating those findings into recommendations that had to hold up in front of people making real decisions with real money on the line.

Right now I'm running Riffd, a music analysis tool I built for musicians who want to understand what's inside a track. Alongside that, I'm developing private systems for market research and automated analysis.

I think carefully about how things are structured — both the product and the code behind it. I'd rather build one thing that works well than five things that sort of work. I care about reliability, deployment, and the experience of actually using something day to day.

Outside of building: music, trading systems, and how information moves through markets.
